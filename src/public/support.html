1. Getting Started

Creating an account

To use UpQ you need to register a free account. Visit the sign-in page and click Register.

1
On the sign-in page, click Register at the bottom of the form.
2
Enter a username, password, and your email address. All three are required.
3
Click Create Account. A verification email will be sent to the address you entered.
4
Open the email and click the Verify my email link. This confirms your address and takes you to the subscription page.
5
Choose a subscription plan and complete checkout. You can start with the free trial — no charges until the trial period ends.
ℹ️ Your account cannot be used until the email address is verified. If you don't see the verification email, check your spam folder. You can request a new one from the sign-in page.

Signing in

Go to the sign-in page, enter your username and password, and click Sign In. You will be taken directly to your task lists.

Resetting a forgotten password

1
On the sign-in page, click Forgot password?
2
Enter the email address associated with your account and click Send Reset Link.
3
Open the email and click the reset link. It expires in 1 hour.
4
Enter and confirm your new password, then click Reset Password. You will be signed in automatically.

3. Features Overview

Task Lists

The Lists view shows all your task lists from every connected provider. If you have both Microsoft Tasks and Google Tasks connected, all lists appear together. Use the filter tabs at the top to view lists from a specific provider. Each list shows a count of remaining tasks.

Managing Tasks

Click any list to open it and see its tasks. From there you can:

  • Create a task — Click + New Task. Enter a name, optional notes, and an optional due date.
  • Complete a task — Click the circle button to the left of the task name. It turns green on hover.
  • Edit a task — Hover over a task and click Edit to change the name, notes, or due date.
  • Delete a task — Hover over a task and click Delete. A confirmation prompt will appear before it is permanently removed.
⚠️ Deleting a task permanently removes it from the provider (Microsoft To Do, Google Tasks, etc.). This cannot be undone.

All Tasks View

The All Tasks view shows tasks from every connected provider and every list in one place. Tasks are grouped and color-coded by their classification.

Task Classification — Now, Next, Later

Every task is automatically classified into one of three buckets:

  • Now — Overdue or high-priority tasks that need immediate attention.
  • Next — Tasks with future due dates or normal priority.
  • Later — Everything else.

Classification is computed automatically on the server each time tasks are loaded. Customizing the rules is a developer feature — see Settings → Danger Zone → Classification Rules to export/import them, or the REST API docs for the full predicate-rule reference.

Settings

The Settings page is where you manage everything about your account:

  • Connect and disconnect Microsoft Tasks and Google Tasks.
  • Set your default provider — the one that loads first when you open UpQ.
  • Toggle Show Completed — when off, completed tasks are excluded from the count shown on each list card.
  • Export, import, or reset your task classification rules.

4. Connecting Microsoft Tasks

UpQ connects to Microsoft To Do using your personal Microsoft account (Outlook, Hotmail, Live, or a Microsoft 365 account). You authorize the connection once and UpQ handles everything from there.

How to connect your Microsoft account

1
Go to Settings in UpQ and find the Microsoft Tasks card.
2
Click Connect. You will be redirected to a Microsoft sign-in page.
3
Sign in with your Microsoft account. If you have multiple accounts, a picker will appear — choose the one that has your tasks.
4
Microsoft will show a permissions consent screen listing what UpQ is requesting:
  • Read and write your tasks
  • Sign you in and read your profile
  • Maintain access to data you have given it access to
Click Accept.
5
You will be returned to UpQ Settings with Microsoft Tasks showing as Connected ✓.
ℹ️ UpQ only accesses your tasks. It does not read your email, calendar, or any other Microsoft data. You can revoke access at any time from your Microsoft account's App permissions page or by clicking Disconnect in UpQ Settings.

Troubleshooting Microsoft connection

  • Wrong account connected — Click Disconnect in Settings, then Connect again. Microsoft will show the account picker so you can choose a different account.
  • Tasks not loading after connecting — Try refreshing the page. If the problem persists, disconnect and reconnect.
  • Consent screen asks for unexpected permissions — UpQ only requests the three permissions listed above. If you see anything else, do not accept and contact support.

Setting up Microsoft API access (administrators only)

This section is for technical administrators setting up a self-hosted UpQ instance. Regular users do not need to do this.

1
Go to portal.azure.com and sign in with a Microsoft account.
2
In the search bar at the top, type App registrations and select it. Click New registration.
3
Give the app a name (e.g. "UpQ Tasks"). Under Supported account types, choose Personal Microsoft accounts only for personal use, or Accounts in any organizational directory and personal accounts for broader access. Click Register.
4
On the app's overview page, copy the Application (client) ID and the Directory (tenant) ID — you'll need these for your .env file.
5
In the left menu, click Manage → API permissions → Add a permission → Microsoft Graph → Delegated permissions. Search for and add: Tasks.ReadWrite, User.Read, and offline_access. Click Add permissions.
6
In the left menu, click Manage → Authentication → Add a platform → Web. Enter your redirect URI (e.g. https://your-domain.com/auth/microsoft/callback) and click Configure.
7
In the left menu, click Manage → Certificates & secrets → New client secret. Give it a description and choose an expiry. Click Add. Copy the Value (not the Secret ID) immediately — it will not be shown again.
8
Add to your .env file:
MICROSOFT_CLIENT_ID=<Application client ID>
MICROSOFT_CLIENT_SECRET=<Secret Value>
MICROSOFT_TENANT_ID=consumers
MICROSOFT_REDIRECT_URI=https://your-domain.com/auth/microsoft/callback
⚠️ The client secret Value is a long string of characters — it is not the GUID shown in the "Secret ID" column. Copy only the Value column.

5. Connecting Google Tasks

UpQ connects to Google Tasks using your Google account. The same tasks you see in Gmail, Google Calendar, or the Google Tasks app will appear in UpQ.

How to connect your Google account

1
Go to Settings in UpQ and find the Google Tasks card.
2
Click Connect. You will be redirected to a Google sign-in page.
3
Sign in with your Google account.
4
Google will show a permissions screen. UpQ requests access to Manage your tasks only. Click Continue.
5
You will be returned to UpQ Settings with Google Tasks showing as Connected ✓.
ℹ️ UpQ only requests access to your tasks — not your email, drive, calendar, or any other Google data. You can revoke access anytime at myaccount.google.com → Security → Third-party apps or by clicking Disconnect in UpQ Settings.

Troubleshooting Google connection

  • Google shows a warning that the app is unverified — This may appear for self-hosted instances. Click Advanced then Go to [app name] (unsafe) to proceed. This is expected for apps that haven't been through Google's verification process.
  • Tasks not loading after connecting — Google Tasks must be enabled in your Google account. Open tasks.google.com to activate it, then reconnect in UpQ Settings.
  • Wrong account connected — Disconnect in Settings, then Connect again and choose a different Google account.

Setting up Google API access (administrators only)

This section is for technical administrators setting up a self-hosted UpQ instance. Regular users do not need to do this.

1
Go to console.cloud.google.com and sign in. Click Select a project → New Project. Give it a name and click Create.
2
In the left menu, go to APIs & Services → Library. Search for Tasks API and click Enable.
3
Go to APIs & Services → OAuth consent screen. Choose External and click Create. Fill in the required fields (App name, support email, developer email). Click Save and Continue through the remaining steps.
4
Go to APIs & Services → Credentials → Create Credentials → OAuth client ID. Set the application type to Web application.
5
Under Authorized redirect URIs, click Add URI and enter your redirect URI (e.g. https://your-domain.com/auth/google/callback). Click Create.
6
A dialog will show your Client ID and Client Secret. Copy both. Add to your .env file:
GOOGLE_CLIENT_ID=<Client ID>
GOOGLE_CLIENT_SECRET=<Client Secret>
GOOGLE_REDIRECT_URI=https://your-domain.com/auth/google/callback
7
On the OAuth consent screen, under Test users, add the Google accounts that will be used for testing. Users must be added here until the app is published.
💡 The redirect URI in Google Cloud Console must match GOOGLE_REDIRECT_URI in your .env exactly — including whether it uses http or https. A mismatch is the most common cause of "redirect_uri_mismatch" errors.

6. Connecting Apple Reminders

Apple Reminders requires a companion app called hb-task-server running on your Mac. It reads your Reminders via AppleScript and connects to UpQ over an encrypted WebSocket. No Apple account credentials are ever sent to the UpQ server.

⚠️ Apple Reminders integration requires a Mac that is running and connected to the internet. If your Mac is off or asleep, Apple tasks will not be accessible.

Setup steps

1
In UpQ Settings, find the Apple Reminders card and click Generate Bridge Key. Copy the key that appears — it is shown only once.
2
Install hb-task-server on your Mac and follow its setup instructions.
3
In hb-task-server's .env file, add:
BRIDGE_URL=wss://tasks.handsbreadth.com/bridge
BRIDGE_API_KEY=<the key you copied in step 1>
4
Start hb-task-server on your Mac. It will connect to UpQ automatically.
5
In UpQ Settings, the Apple Reminders card should now show a Connected status. Set Apple as your default provider if you wish.
